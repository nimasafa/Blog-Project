George Washington

Born in 1732 into a Virginia planter family, George Washington was elected Commander in Chief of the Continental Army in 1775, charged with leading ill-trained troops against a formidable, organized British army. After defeating the British in Yorktown, he was subsequently unanimously elected by the Electoral College to serve as the first President of the United States.
